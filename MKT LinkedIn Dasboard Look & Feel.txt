Dashboard Look and Feel
Style Direction
The dashboard should feel professional but fun.
It should look polished enough to share publicly as part of my “Summer of AI GTM” learning project.
The design should feel:
* Warm
* Clean
* Modern
* Marketer-friendly
* Friendly
* Confident
* Easy to read
* Useful, not overly technical
It should not feel like a plain spreadsheet or a dark corporate SaaS dashboard.
Color Palette
Use a summer-inspired color palette:
* Maroon
* Sienna
* Cream
* Mustard
* Soft neutral backgrounds
The design should use these colors in a tasteful way.
Suggested usage:
* Cream or soft neutral for the page background
* Maroon for key headings or primary accents
* Sienna for secondary accents
* Mustard for highlights, badges, chart accents, or key callouts
* Soft neutrals for cards and section backgrounds
Visual Elements
Use interesting but simple visual elements:
* Summary cards
* Rounded cards
* Soft shadows
* Circular stat cards
* Progress-style circles
* Simple graphs
* Clear tables or post cards
* Friendly empty states
* Clear section dividers
* Warm visual accents
The dashboard should feel visual and engaging without being busy.
Layout
Recommended layout:
1. Header section with dashboard title and short description.
2. CSV upload area near the top.
3. Overall Summary section with metric cards.
4. Visual Performance section with charts.
5. Performance by Post section with cards or a table.
6. Recommendations section at the bottom.
Tone of the Interface
Use clear, friendly labels.
Examples:
* Overall Summary
* Performance by Post
* Topic Performance
* Best Performing Post
* Upload New CSV
* Recommendations
* What to Post Next
Avoid developer-heavy language.
Avoid labels that sound too technical.
Avoid
Avoid:
* Plain spreadsheet look
* Developer-heavy interface
* Dark corporate SaaS styling
* Overly complex menus
* Tiny fonts
* Too many charts
* Anything too busy or hard to read
* Anything childish or cartoonish
The dashboard should feel like something a smart marketer would proudly share on LinkedIn.
